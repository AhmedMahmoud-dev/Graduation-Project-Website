Emotra's audio emotion engine is a multimodal system — it doesn't just listen to what you say, it also analyzes how you say it. The pipeline combines speech-to-text transcription with voice tone analysis to produce a fused emotion result that captures both linguistic and acoustic emotional cues.

The first step is transcription. Emotra uses Whisper (small), OpenAI's automatic speech recognition model, to convert audio recordings into text. Whisper handles background noise, different accents, and variable speaking speeds.

Model: whisper-small
Sample Rate: 16,000 Hz

The audio waveform is analyzed using a sliding window approach. A 3-second window moves over the audio with a 1-second hop, producing an emotion prediction for every segment. This creates a timeline of emotions across the recording.

The HuBERT model only recognizes 4 emotions: neutral, joy, anger, sadness. To match Emotra's 7-class system, we use a synthetic budget allocation strategy — 90% of the probability goes to the real emotions, and 10% is distributed to the 3 derived emotions.

The final step merges the two emotion channels — what was said (text) and how it was expressed (voice tone) — using a balanced 50/50 fusion. This ensures both channels have equal influence on the final emotion result.

Each emotion probability is the simple average of its audio and text counterpart. If the text API fails, audio probabilities are used as fallback.

The API returns a rich multimodal result: the transcribed text, per-segment emotion timeline from the audio channel, text emotion analysis results, the fused multimodal emotion, and metadata about all three models used.

Only 4 Native Audio Emotions

HuBERT natively detects only neutral, joy, anger, and sadness. Disgust, fear, and surprise are synthetically derived and may be less accurate.

Audio Quality Dependency

Background noise, music, or poor microphone quality can significantly reduce accuracy for both transcription and emotion detection.

Accent Sensitivity

Whisper performs best with standard accents. Heavy accents or non-native English speakers may result in transcription errors that affect text emotion accuracy.

No Music or Noise Handling

The model is designed for speech. Audio with music, sound effects, or multiple speakers may produce unreliable results.
